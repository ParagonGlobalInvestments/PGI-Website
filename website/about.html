Who we are

Paragon National Group is a student-led 501(c)(3) non-profit organization with chapters at the University of Chicago and the University of Pennsylvania. The organization is dedicated to the development of students in value investing, computer and data science, and quantitative finance.
Students admitted to PNG follow an educational curriculum that teaches the fundamentals of investing, programming, and quantitative financial analysis.

Once completed with the educational portion, students will join the organization either as inestment analysts or quantitative analysts, working on stock pitches and projects the will be invested and used directly into an actively managed portfolio of publicly traded stocks directly through PNG's 20,000 dollar investment fund.

Value Investing

Part 1 of the curriculum will help students gain a basic understanding behind the theory and strategies of value investing. Students will gain a fundamental understanding of accounting, competitive analysis, and valuation. General modeling, including operating models and discounted cash flows models, will be explored as well as general investment theory related to these models. By the end of the curriculum, students will be able to accurately value companies and understand complex business models.

Computer and Data Science

Part 2 of the curriculum aims to educate students on the theory and applications of computer and data science. The curriculum will revolve around the Python programming language. Students will learn underlying theory of computer systems, basic programming in Python, and applied data science using Pandas and Numpy. An emphasis will be on applied statistical analysis of financial data. Students will also learn to create complex data visualization dashboards using Streamlit and Plotly.

Quantitative Finance

Part 3 of the curriculum focuses on educating students on applied forms of Quantitative Portfolio Management, utilizing the information learned in the first 2 parts of the curriculum. Students will learn the basics of modern portfolio theory including analysis of systematic and idiosyncratic risks and total return analysis using the Sharpe, Info, and Treynor ratios. Eventually students will learn to construct a portfolio based off the Mean-Variance Analysis. Basic statistics, linear algebra, and applied regression analysis will be taught in conjunction with this portion. Later, factor regression analysis will be taught with a focus on systematic portfolio construction and smart beta.
