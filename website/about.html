Who we are

The Paragon National Group is an organization dedicated to the development of students in both value investing fundamentals and in an understanding of technology, computer programming, and data science. We believe that an understanding of computer and data science is pivotal in the current business environment as technology is becoming integrated into every sector and the demand of analysts who can code is constantly increasing.

Curriculum

Fundamentals

This portion of the course will focus on teaching students the fundamentals of accounting, finance, and valuation. More info coming soon.

Computer and Data Science

Part 2 of the curriculum aims to educate students on the theory and applications of computer and data science. The curriculum will revolve around the Python programming language. Students will learn underlying theory of computer systems, basic programming in python, and applied data science using Pandas, Numpy, and Matplotlib. Topics will focus on teaching the students syntax and uses of basic python and will also explore more advanced concepts like Object Orientated Programming, Input and Output, Web Scraping, and more. The Data Science curriculum will teach students how to analyze and manipulate data to derive complex statistical analyses from data tables. Using Pandas, Numpy, Matplotlib, etc., our students will be able to collect, clean, and analyze raw data to create meaningful reports and draw quality conclusions by implementing the basics of Python and R.

Quantitative Portfolio Management

Part 3 of the curriculum focuses on educating students on applied forms of Quantitative Portfolio Management using the information learned in the first 2 parts of the curriculum. Students will learn the basic of modern portfolio theory including analysis of systematic and idiosyncratic risks and total return analysis using the Sharpe, Info, and Treynor ratios. Eventually students will learn to construct a portfolio based off the Mean-Variance Analysis and its variations. Basic statistics, linear algebra, and applied regression analysis will be taught in conjunction with this portion. Later, factor analysis and investing will be taught with a focus on smart beta, hedging, and tracking assets. This conclusion to the course will teach students not only to pick the best assets, but to also analyze their portfolio allocation to optimize their portfolio returns.
